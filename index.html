üìã DD/C Method Overview

What is DD/C?

The Dual Discrepancy/Consistency (DD/C) method is based on the most salient diagnostic markers of SLD that have been prevalent in the literature for decades. It is grounded in the Cattell-Horn-Carroll (CHC) theory and important neuropsychological functions.

üîç First Discrepancy

An aggregate of cognitive processing and ability strengths is used to predict expected academic performance. When academic performance falls below this prediction, it supports emergence of underachievement.

üîç Second Discrepancy

An aggregate of cognitive processing and ability strengths is used to predict expected cognitive performance. Base rates determine if the difference supports a characteristic cognitive weakness.

‚úÖ Consistency Analysis

An analysis determines whether there are below average cognitive and academic scores that are empirically related. This determines Consistency between related cognitive or processing and academic SLD weaknesses.

Why "Dual Discrepancy/Consistency"?

DUAL DISCREPANCY (the "DD" part)

There are TWO required discrepancies:

  • First: Between general cognitive ability and specific cognitive weakness(es)
  • Second: Between general cognitive ability and academic weakness(es)

Both discrepancies must be statistically significant.

CONSISTENCY (the "C" part)

The cognitive weakness and academic weakness should be consistent (related/aligned) with each other. Example: A weakness in phonological processing (cognitive) is consistent with a weakness in reading decoding (academic).

Best Practices

  • Multiple Data Sources: Use prereferral intervention info, parent/teacher reports, observations
  • Clinical Judgment: Consider quantitative data within entire case history context
  • Not Automatic: Meeting criteria doesn't automatically confirm or rule out SLD
  • Multiple Measures: Use multiple measures of constructs rather than single subtests
  • X-BASS Required: Software needed for proper DD/C analysis

üìä Five Levels of DD/C Evaluation

Level I: Academic Achievement Assessment

Establishes whether a normative weakness or deficit exists in academic performance.

Classification: Weakness (85-89) | Deficit (<85)
Required for SLD

Level II: Exclusionary Factors Examination

Investigates alternative explanations: intellectual disability, lack of motivation, limited English proficiency, environmental disadvantage, medical conditions.

Key: SLD only ruled out when factors are primarily responsible for difficulties.

Prevents Misidentification

Level III: Cognitive Abilities Assessment

Evaluates broad and narrow cognitive abilities and neuropsychological processes essential for SLD identification.

Same criteria as Level I: Weakness (85-89) | Deficit (<85)
CHC Based

Level IV: PSW Analysis

Uses X-BASS 2.0 to determine DD/C pattern. ALL FIVE criteria must be met:

  1. General intelligence ‚â•90
  2. At least one cognitive ability weakness (<90)
  3. Significant difference between general ability and cognitive weakness
  4. At least one academic weakness (<90)
  5. Significant difference between general intelligence and academic weakness
X-BASS Required Core of DD/C

Level V: Multidisciplinary Team Decision

Team determines whether students meeting DD/C criteria require special education services. Ensures identification translates into appropriate educational support.
