üìã DD/C Method Overview

What is DD/C?

The Dual Discrepancy/Consistency (DD/C) method is based on the most salient diagnostic markers of SLD that have been prevalent in the literature for decades. It is grounded in the Cattell-Horn-Carroll (CHC) theory and important neuropsychological functions.

üîç First Discrepancy

An aggregate of cognitive processing and ability strengths is used to predict expected academic performance. When academic performance falls below this prediction, it supports emergence of underachievement.

üîç Second Discrepancy

An aggregate of cognitive processing and ability strengths is used to predict expected cognitive performance. Base rates determine if the difference supports a characteristic cognitive weakness.

‚úÖ Consistency Analysis

An analysis determines whether there are below average cognitive and academic scores that are empirically related. This determines Consistency between related cognitive or processing and academic SLD weaknesses.

Why "Dual Discrepancy/Consistency"?

DUAL DISCREPANCY (the "DD" part)

There are TWO required discrepancies:

  • First: Between general cognitive ability and specific cognitive weakness(es)
  • Second: Between general cognitive ability and academic weakness(es)

Both discrepancies must be statistically significant.

CONSISTENCY (the "C" part)

The cognitive weakness and academic weakness should be consistent (related/aligned) with each other. Example: A weakness in phonological processing (cognitive) is consistent with a weakness in reading decoding (academic).

Best Practices

  • Multiple Data Sources: Use prereferral intervention info, parent/teacher reports, observations
  • Clinical Judgment: Consider quantitative data within entire case history context
  • Not Automatic: Meeting criteria doesn't automatically confirm or rule out SLD
  • Multiple Measures: Use multiple measures of constructs rather than single subtests
  • X-BASS Required: Software needed for proper DD/C analysis

Levels of Evaluation in the DD/C PSW Method

Level I: Academic Achievement Assessment

This initial level establishes whether a normative weakness or deficit exists in academic performance‚Äîa necessary but insufficient condition for SLD determination. Students are evaluated using standardized, norm-referenced achievement tests (M=100, SD=15), with results corroborated by additional data sources like parent and teacher reports.

Classification criteria:
  • Weakness: Standard score between 85-89
  • Deficit: Standard score below 85

Clinical judgment is permitted in borderline cases. If no weakness or deficit is found, the student cannot qualify for SLD under this method.

Required for SLD

Level II: Exclusionary Factors Examination

This level investigates alternative explanations for underachievement beyond cognitive processing disorders, including:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Lack of motivation
  • Limited English proficiency
  • Environmental/economic disadvantage
  • Medical conditions (hearing/vision disorders)

Critical distinction: SLD is only ruled out when these factors are **primarily responsible** for academic difficulties. If they merely co-occur with or contribute to underachievement, SLD remains a viable explanation. This level prevents misidentification rather than automatically excluding students.

Prevents Misidentification

Level III: Cognitive Abilities Assessment

This level evaluates broad and narrow cognitive abilities and neuropsychological processes considered essential for SLD identification. The DD/C method requires evidence of cognitive weakness or deficit underlying academic difficulties, using the same scoring criteria as Level I (weakness: 85-89; deficit: <85, with clinical judgment allowed).

**Key principle:** Without identified cognitive weaknesses or deficits, an essential SLD criterion remains unmet, preventing classification.

CHC Based

Level IV: Pattern of Strengths and Weaknesses (PSW) Analysis

This analytical level uses the **Cross-Battery Assessment Software System (X-BASS 2.0)** to determine if a DD/C pattern exists within an otherwise normal cognitive profile. After excluding alternative explanations, SLD is identified when **all five** criteria are met:

  1. General intelligence ‚â•90 (average or higher)
  2. At least one broad cognitive ability weakness (standard score <90)
  3. Statistically significant difference between general ability and cognitive weakness(es)
  4. At least one academic achievement weakness (standard score <90)
  5. Statistically significant difference between general intelligence and academic weakness(es)

**Notable feature:** While the difference between cognitive and academic weaknesses is tested statistically, a non-significant result is neither required for inclusion nor grounds for exclusion.

X-BASS Required Core of DD/C

Level V: Multidisciplinary Team Decision

The final level involves a multidisciplinary team meeting to determine whether students meeting the DD/C criteria require special education and related services. This ensures that identification translates into appropriate educational support.

Final Determination
